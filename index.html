If you're tired of hiding your smile, struggling with uncomfortable dentures, or missing out on the foods you love, dental implants can help. Implant options

Dental implant solutions we offer

Each option is a long-term solution for missing teeth, selected based on your oral health, the number of teeth being replaced, and your goals.

Most common

Single tooth implants

Replace one missing tooth with a fixed, natural-looking implant — without relying on or affecting the healthy teeth either side.

  • Permanent, fixed replacement
  • Preserves the surrounding teeth

Multiple implants

Strategically placed implants to replace several missing teeth — more stable and longer-lasting than a removable denture.

  • Replaces several gaps at once
  • No impact on healthy teeth
Full arch

All-on-4 full arch

A full set of fixed teeth supported by just four implants — ideal when most or all teeth in an arch are missing.

  • A whole arch on four implants
  • Fixed — not a removable denture

Implant-supported dentures

Anchor your denture to implants for far less movement — no more slipping, clicking or messy adhesives.

  • Greater stability than a standard denture
  • A more secure long-term solution

Good to know

Common questions

Many people with missing or damaged teeth are suitable, including those finding dentures uncomfortable. Suitability depends on your oral health, bone structure and individual circumstances, so a consultation is required to determine whether implant treatment is appropriate for you.

Most patients experience minimal discomfort. Your dentist will talk you through exactly what to expect at each stage of your treatment, so you feel informed and at ease throughout.

The investment depends on how many teeth are being replaced and the treatment that suits you, confirmed at your consultation. We offer flexible payment plans, interest-free options (subject to approval), and can guide eligible patients on early release of super — with all fees discussed clearly before anything begins.

Eligible patients may be able to access their superannuation early to help fund dental treatment. Eligibility is decided by the relevant authorities, not the practice, so we recommend seeking independent advice. We're happy to explain how the process generally works.

Timeframes vary depending on healing requirements and the complexity of your individual case. Your personalised plan will set out what to expect from start to finish.
